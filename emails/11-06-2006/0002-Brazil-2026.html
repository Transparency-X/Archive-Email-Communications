Documented Vulnerabilities in Ireland

Our preliminary research indicates that Brazilians in Ireland face distinct vulnerabilities that often fall outside traditional consular reporting metrics:

• Labor Exploitation: Undocumented workers and Stamp 2 visa holders face disproportionate wage theft, with fear of deportation preventing formal complaints.
• Housing Precarity: Overcrowding and illegal subletting targeting new arrivals, exacerbated by Ireland’s housing crisis and lack of tenancy protections.
• Barriers to Justice: Language barriers and immigration-status-dependent access to services create severe underreporting of assault and workplace abuse.
• Intersectional Risks: LGBTQI+ Brazilians and racialized migrants face compounded risks of discrimination rarely captured in aggregate statistics.

How the Framework Supports Your Mission

The Framework provides diplomatic posts with tools to enhance protective outcomes while reducing administrative burden:

  • Standardized Harm Taxonomy: A unified classification system enabling consistent cross-jurisdictional data comparison.
  • Multi-Scale Impact Assessment: Tools to evaluate harm at individual, family, and community levels.
  • Evidence-Based Reporting: Pre-validated formats aligned with international human rights standards, streamlining communication with Brasília.
  • Stakeholder Mapping: Curated directories of Irish civil society, legal aid, and migrant support organizations.
